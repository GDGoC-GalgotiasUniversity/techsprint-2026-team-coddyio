## Simple Spray Solutions

### Baking Soda Spray
- **What:** 1 spoon baking soda + 1 bucket water
- **When:** For leaf spots, mold, rust
- **How:** Mix well, spray on leaves
- **How Often:** Every 7-10 days

### Milk Spray
- **What:** 1 part milk + 9 parts water
- **When:** For early blight, mold
- **How:** Mix well, spray on leaves
- **How Often:** Every 7-10 days

### Cooking Oil Spray
- **What:** Few drops cooking oil + bucket water
- **When:** For rust, spots, insects
- **How:** Mix well, spray on leaves
- **How Often:** Every 10-14 days

## Simple Techniques

### Remove Bad Leaves
- Pick leaves by hand
- Throw them away (don't compost)
- Do this early morning or evening
- Helps stop disease spreading

### Cut Branches
- Cut branches to let air flow
- Remove branches touching ground
- Remove dead branches
- Helps leaves dry faster

### Mulch (Dry Grass)
- Put dry grass around plant base
- Keeps soil from splashing on leaves
- Keeps soil moist
- Stops disease spreading from soil

### Watering Right
- Water the soil, NOT the leaves
- Water early morning
- Water at plant base
- Wet leaves help disease grow

## When to Use What

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Spots on leaves | Baking soda spray |
| White powder on leaves | Milk spray |
| Rust-colored spots | Cooking oil spray |
| Leaves touching ground | Remove them |
| Crowded plants | Cut branches |
| Wet leaves | Water soil only |
| Bare soil | Add dry grass |

## Prevention Tips

### Every Week
- Check plants for bad leaves
- Remove any bad leaves
- Check soil moisture

### Every Month
- Spray preventive solution
- Cut branches for air flow
- Add more dry grass if needed

### Every Season
- Plant in different spot
- Use disease-resistant varieties
- Clean up old plant material
